Projects

Carpenter Center Study Space

The Carpenter Center for Visual Arts was built by Swiss-French Architect, Le Corbusier, and is located on Harvard University’s campus. For this project, students had to design and build a learning space in the northeast façade of the building.

Pavilion

Students had to design and build a public pavilion with different apertures to highlight its environment. In addition, students had to showcase two prominent sculptures from the Museum of Fine Arts (Boston, MA) through different architectural motifs.

Museum Stairs

Students had to design and build a museum from a preexisting armature, featuring two paintings found in the Museum of Fine Arts (Boston, MA). The design needed to include vertical circulation and premeditated apertures in order to curate the way patrons view the paintings.

Free Plan Design

After studying works by Juliaan Lampens, Le Corbusier, and Ludwig Mies van der Rohe, students had to design and build houses following free plan conventions. Designs must contain programmatic elements to act as service and living spaces and cannot exceed 1,100 square feet.

Raum Plan Design

After studying works by Adolf Loos, students had to design and build houses following Raumplan conventions. Designs must contain programmatic elements with a logical vertical circulation and cannot exceed 2,000 square feet.

Thick Thin Design

After studying works by Pezo von Ellrichshausen, students had to design and build houses following thick-thin conventions. Designs must contain programmatic elements with the perception of “thick” and “thin spaces” and cannot exceed 2,000 square feet.
